Julien Jumper

Julien Jumper was one of the hardest projects I had to work on during my school term. We were required to use the Direct X 11/12 or Vulkan APK to create a playable experience and our answer unlike any other member of our class was a 2d vertical platformer. Everyone in my group did our previous project in Direct X 11 leaving me the only member with any Vulkan experience.
This was the first of two projects that I took the Director role for. At the time my understanding of Direct X 11 and other APKs was too weak to keep up with the powerhouses I was given. So I was given the Director position and Team Lead position since I was the only member with enough time to work around everyone's hectic schedules. I remember many late nights and early mornings discussing with Liam and Kevin about where we were with the project. Liam had become our FLECS master, Unfortunately we never needed to use it for our project and were given the OK by the producers to not use it.
The chaos of that first meeting where we began joking with each other until someone brought up the movie "Madagascar" and that cemented the idea of our main character being a lemur named Julien. We decided on a platformer, despite our producers warning about the difficulty with collision checking. We really should have taken those few extra minutes as our collision system was finished minutes before our final week. On the bright side that knowledge of working on collision systems for 3 weeks came in handy when I had three members of this group during the production of Megadude.
Keeping up with a team where two members are only available in the evening and one who was only available at night did add some difficulty in relaying everyones progress. I set up branches in the Git Repo so everyone was able to work effectively. Whenever an issue arose that was when the Tyrant of Chaos would come to assist in brainstorming, code review, or when necessary debug it myself. This was the month were I had to learn BASH we had some moments where you could swear that Git itself was working against us. Between staring at merge conflicts almost every day and that one day when I manually parsed all of our code base before giving up and using Bash to overwrite the debugging branch.
